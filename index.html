Because 19 hour 4 day marathons with rigid schedules and repetitive commercials are an inalienable human (and martian) right. You think about that now.

What Is this

The annual 4th of July Twilight Zone marathon on Syfy Sci-Fi (Never give up. Never surrender) has been a Kerry family tradition for as long as I can remember. Critically important elements of this tradition include:

  • Opening and being aware of the episode schedule at all times.
  • Calling / texting / yelling at other family members when key episodes fire.
  • Cursing the day that Holes 99786989796 was created since you've listened to the commercial for the Nth time, completely melding it into your subconscious.
  • Lamenting the midnight ball drop, since it will require you halt your addiction for normal human activities.
  • Loathing the regularly scheduled programming and infomercials that cause temporary lapses in coverage.

When my wife and I decided to cut the cord last year we didn't realize the horror we were creating for ourselves. Our error wasn't obvious until that year's Twilight zone marathon plans started rolling in. Yes, you could just binge it all on Netflix like a casual. You could spread it over a week. You could just watch a few at your mom's place. My friends- that would make you Obsolete.
